A Universally Unique Identifier (UUID) is a 128-bit number used to uniquely identify information in computer systems. Version 4 UUIDs are based on random numbers.

### Features

- Generate single or batch UUID v4 identifiers
- Copy all generated UUIDs at once
- Follows RFC 4122 standards

### How to Use

1. Set the quantity of UUIDs you need.
2. Click "Generate UUIDs" to create them.
3. Use "Copy All" to copy all generated UUIDs to your clipboard.

### Why UUIDs?

- Unique across space and time (no central coordination needed)
- Ideal for database primary keys
- Perfect for distributed system identifiers

### Privacy

All UUIDs are generated locally using cryptographically strong randomness. No data is sent anywhere.
